🎯 Core Philosophy

Why Choose Growth-Hybrid?

The growth-hybrid strategy recognizes that while high monthly income is attractive, preserving and growing capital is equally important for long-term wealth building. This approach aims to:

Generate Meaningful Income

Target 50-75% annual yield for substantial monthly cash flow

📈

Participate in market upside through growth allocations

⚖️

🔄

Compound Returns

🛡️

Preserve Capital

Reduce NAV erosion risk through diversification

🎯

Flexibility

Adjust allocations based on market conditions

✨ Key Advantages

1. Sustainable Income Generation

By limiting high-yield exposure to 50%, the strategy reduces dependency on volatile option premiums while still generating meaningful monthly income.

2. Capital Preservation

Growth allocations help offset potential NAV erosion from covered call strategies, providing a buffer during market downturns.

3. Flexibility in Market Conditions

  • Bull Markets: Growth allocation captures upside while covered calls limit income ETF gains
  • Sideways Markets: Perfect for covered call strategies, maximizing income generation
  • Bear Markets: Multiple income sources provide cushion, rebalancing opportunities

4. Tax Efficiency Options

Ability to harvest losses in growth positions to offset ordinary income from distributions. Strategic asset location between taxable and tax-advantaged accounts.

📋 Your Action Plan

Step 1: Determine Your Income Needs

Calculate minimum required monthly income. The growth-hybrid strategy typically generates $4,000-5,000 monthly per $100,000 invested.

Step 2: Choose Your Allocation

Start with 50/30/20 (Income/Growth/Dividend) and adjust based on:

  • Age and time horizon
  • Risk tolerance
  • Income requirements
  • Tax situation

Step 3: Select Specific Holdings

Choose 3-5 YieldMax ETFs for income, 2-3 growth ETFs, and 2-3 dividend growth funds. Avoid over-diversification.

Step 4: Implement Gradually

Deploy capital over 3-6 months to average into positions. Start with core income holdings.

Step 5: Set Rebalancing Schedule

Review quarterly, rebalance when any allocation drifts more than 5% from target.
