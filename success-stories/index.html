From $0 to $12,000 Monthly Income in 18 Months

After retiring from a 30-year engineering career, I was looking for ways to generate consistent income without depleting my retirement savings. Traditional dividend stocks were yielding 2-4%, which wasn't enough to maintain my lifestyle.

I discovered YieldMax ETFs in early 2023 through a finance forum. Initially skeptical of the high yields, I started small with a $50,000 investment split between TSLY and NVDY. The first monthly distribution exceeded my expectations.

Key Milestone: By month 6, I was generating $3,500 in monthly income. I gradually increased my position and diversified across 8 different YieldMax funds.

Today, my YieldMax portfolio of $320,000 generates approximately $12,000 per month. Yes, there's volatility, and I've seen my principal fluctuate by 20-30%, but the consistent monthly income has been life-changing.

My Top Lessons:

  1. Start small and learn how these funds behave in different market conditions
  2. Don't panic during principal drawdowns - focus on the income
  3. Diversify across multiple funds to reduce single-stock risk
  4. Keep 6-12 months of expenses in cash as a buffer
  5. Reinvest 20-30% of distributions during market dips

Top Tips from Our Community

Wisdom shared by successful YieldMax investors

📊

Start with Education

Understand covered calls and how YieldMax generates income before investing. Knowledge reduces panic during volatility.

🎯

Set Income Goals

Calculate exactly how much monthly income you need and work backwards to determine portfolio size.

⚖️

Diversify Holdings

Don't put all eggs in one fund. Spread across 5-10 different YieldMax ETFs to reduce single-stock risk.

💰

Cash Reserve

Keep 6-12 months of expenses in cash. This prevents forced selling during market downturns.

📈

Reinvest Wisely

Consider reinvesting 20-30% of distributions, especially during market dips, to grow your income over time.

🛡️

Risk Management

Never invest more than you can afford to see drop 30-40%. Principal volatility is real with these funds.
